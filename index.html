ABOUT ME

Hiya. I live in San Francisco and am a software engineer interested in front-end web development, with a little backend thrown in now and then to keep things intersting. I changed careers a few years ago, from an engineer of the physical realm to an engineer of the virtual realm, so to speak.

In my first development role, building the front-end of a cordova app and internal and enterprise desktop apps at Ziploop, I mastered the essentials of web development and building a great user experience. I earned the trust of the President and CTO and was creating design sketches in Illustator and coding them up in Angular. When I needed a change of pace I worked in our node-mongo backend, super satisfying to get involved in the entire stack from ideation to api.

I'm currently at Talentbin by Monster taking a slightly different turn to beef up my experience in a different tech stack (java-php-javascript) and help bring some legacy code into the future. I'm working with a supportive team that stresses learning and collaborative coding and I'm enjoying the exposure to great tech discussions and the opportunities to implement cleaner, more fun, maintainable front-end architecture.

My programming experience spans my background in Materials Science and Computational Fluid Dynamics, topics of my Ph.D. research at UC Berkeley, in which I modeled liquid metal systems. Programming was one of the most enjoyable parts of my engineering career: writing custom functions to extend commercial simulation software, data analysis in Matlab, Scheme scripts for automatizing post-processing visualization of fluid flow simulations — I can even recall a few FORTRAN scripts in Engineering classes. :)

What was missing in my academic career was a personal connection to the systems I was analyzing. Enter JavaScript and web development. I realized that I could take my programming experience and apply it to build something visual and personally tangible.
